# Phase 4.3 UI Review

## Scope

- Surface audited: `Dashboard Executivo`
- URL: `https://saas-frontend-pearl.vercel.app/dashboard/executive`
- Date: `2026-03-26`
- Method: live pilot inspection + source review

## Score Summary

- Copywriting: `2/4`
- Visuals: `3/4`
- Color: `3/4`
- Typography: `3/4`
- Spacing: `3/4`
- Experience Design: `2/4`

**Overall:** `16/24`

## What Works

- Desktop layout looks like a real product, not a raw admin template.
- Primary executive KPIs are visible near the top.
- Main drill-down actions are discoverable.
- Navigation to `Retention` and `Operational` works.
- Mobile keeps a single-column layout without horizontal overflow.

## Top Fixes

1. Remove or replace dead top-bar search on executive dashboard.
2. Add trust context to KPIs: period, freshness, and why values can be zero.
3. Deep-link priority rows to the specific member/context instead of generic dashboards.
4. Fix mixed-content notifications request in production.
5. Rework executive narrative so it is less operational and less duplicated.

## Findings By Pillar

### Copywriting

- Many labels are generic or mixed-language:
  - `Dashboard`
  - `Workspace`
  - `Settings`
  - `Help Center`
  - `NEW`
- Executive summary text is understandable but too operational.
- The manual executive summary and AI insight repeat the same message.

### Visuals

- Visual shell is polished and cohesive.
- The chart area looks empty when values are zero or near-zero, which feels broken.
- ROI card competes weakly for attention and does not clearly justify its position.

### Color

- Base palette is consistent and premium.
- Semantic colors are used well in badges.
- The chart lines and grid have low visual salience for decision-making.

### Typography

- Headings are readable and hierarchy is mostly correct.
- Several labels in Portuguese are stripped of accents, which hurts local polish.
- Dense lists at the bottom become harder to scan on mobile.

### Spacing

- Desktop spacing is solid and consistent.
- Mobile stacking is functional but long and tiring.
- Prime area in the top bar is wasted by a disabled search field.

### Experience Design

- The page answers "what is happening" better than "what changed" and "what to do next".
- Action rows are not true drill-downs; they open generic dashboards.
- No clear freshness label, timeframe, or metric definitions reduce trust.

## Bugs Observed

### Mixed-content notifications

- Severity: `high`
- Reproduction:
  - open executive dashboard on production
  - inspect browser console/network
- Behavior:
  - notifications request is duplicated, and one copy hits `http://ai-gym-os-api-production.up.railway.app/...`
  - browser blocks it as mixed content
- Impact:
  - notification count/trust can break in a page already used by leadership
- Recommended fix:
  - audit runtime origin used by notification polling in production and remove any insecure fallback

### Priority row loses context

- Severity: `medium`
- Reproduction:
  - click `Abrir` on any row in `Acoes prioritarias`
- Behavior:
  - row opens a generic dashboard, not the specific member or filtered queue item
- Impact:
  - weakens the usefulness of the executive panel as a decision launcher
- Recommended fix:
  - deep-link to member drawer, retention queue filter, or operational filter with preserved context

## Product Risks

- `R$ 0` revenue with `1456` active members strongly hurts credibility without explanation.
- `0.0%` churn across all months can be interpreted as broken or fake data.
- Weekly summary is useful, but without period labels on the main KPIs executives can misread scope.
- The dashboard still feels closer to "operations overview" than "executive cockpit".

## Recommendation

Do not redesign from scratch. Keep the visual shell, but tighten the narrative:

- make the top strip explicitly answer business health
- collapse duplicated insight areas into one strong summary block
- turn action rows into real drill-downs
- add data freshness and period context
- treat zero-value finance/churn states as contextual warnings, not silent facts
